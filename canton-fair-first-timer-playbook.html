The Canton Fair, Demystified: A First-Timer's Playbook

Mar 10, 2026 · 4 min read

Three phases, 40+ halls and 25,000 exhibitors. How to prep, what to bring, and how we help clients walk it efficiently.

Pick the Right Phase

Phase 1 (Apr/Oct): Electronics, machinery, building materials. Phase 2: Consumer goods, decoration. Phase 3: Textiles, food, medical. Choose based on your category — don't spread thin.

Pre-Register Online

Skip the on-site queue. Buyer card costs roughly RMB 100. Bring two passport-size photos and your business card.

Plan Your Halls Day-by-Day

Each phase covers 3.4 million sqm. Walking randomly burns days. Pre-mark 30–50 booths from the online directory before you arrive.

Bring Real Specifications

Vague enquiries get vague quotes. Bring drawings, target specs, target volumes and target FOB price ranges. Suppliers respect prepared buyers.

Schedule Factory Visits Smart

Most exhibitors offer factory tours for serious buyers. Cluster visits geographically — Foshan factories together, Dongguan together, etc.

After the Fair

Within one week, send formal RFQs to your top 5 suppliers per category. Most leads go cold by week three. We handle this follow-up for clients in real time.

Need help putting this into practice?

We do this for our clients every day — from supplier audits to consolidation to compliance. Talk to our Guangzhou team about your project.
